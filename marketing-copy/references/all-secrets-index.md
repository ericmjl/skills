# All 31 secrets index

*Frameworks adapted from Jim Edwards' Copywriting Secrets.*

Quick lookup of all 31 secrets, grouped by theme. Each entry gives a one-sentence takeaway and points to the reference file that covers it in depth. This is an index, not a deep dive.

## Foundations (Secrets 1 to 5)

### 1. What Is Copywriting?

Copywriting is any message meant to persuade the right person to take one specific action; it is defined by intention, not format.
*See: `psychology-and-emotion.md`*

### 2. One Man's Journey With Sales Copy

Copywriting is a learnable skill, not an innate gift; mastering it can transform a business.
*See: overview (mindset)*

### 3. Without A Strong Why, People Don't Buy

People buy for one of ten reasons (make money, save money, save time, avoid effort, escape pain, and more); tie multiple reasons to every offer.
*See: `psychology-and-emotion.md`*

### 4. Nobody Cares About You

Readers care about themselves; scrub "I, me, my, we, ours" and reframe to "you, your, yours."
*See: `psychology-and-emotion.md`*

### 5. The Most Valuable Skill You'll Ever Learn

Learn copywriting yourself for speed, independence, and the ability to judge good copy, even if you also outsource.
*See: overview (mindset)*

## Core skills (Secrets 6 to 12)

### 6. The Most Important Piece of Sales Copy

The headline determines everything; a great headline with mediocre copy beats excellent copy with a weak headline.
*See: `headlines-and-bullets.md`*

### 7. It's Never One Size Fits All

Match the message to hot, warm, and cold traffic; different awareness levels need different angles.
*See: `headlines-and-bullets.md`*

### 8. Meet F.R.E.D. (Your Ideal Customer)

Define your avatar's psychographics (fears, desires, end goals, dreams) in their own words before writing.
*See: `fred-avatar.md`*

### 9. The Ultimate Bullet Formula

Bullets sell on benefit, not feature; convert every feature into what it does for the buyer.
*See: `headlines-and-bullets.md`*

### 10. What Really Sells People

Features become benefits become meaning; meaning creates the emotion that actually triggers the purchase.
*See: `psychology-and-emotion.md`*

### 11. Why Good Enough Makes You Poor

A/B test relentlessly; small conversion gains compound into large profit, and "good enough" keeps you poor.
*See: `review-checklist.md`*

### 12. Don't Reinvent The Wheel

Research what already works (Amazon reviews, competitor funnels, customer language) and model it.
*See: `swipe-and-polish.md`*

## Writing copy (Secrets 13 to 20)

### 13. It's All About Them

Write in the reader's own words at a simple reading level; a confused mind always says no.
*See: `psychology-and-emotion.md`*

### 14. What To Do If You Don't Have Testimonials Yet

The real need is proof; substitute endorsements, statistics, quotes, demonstrations, and free samples.
*See: `proof-and-closes.md`*

### 15. 3 Sales Formulas That Never Fail

Build on reliable structures: Problem-Agitate-Solve, benefit-led, and Before-After-Bridge.
*See: `sales-formulas.md`*

### 16. It's All Ice Cream, But What Flavor?

Pick the right letter format (video, long, or short) for the job; start with a video sales letter to test fastest.
*See: `sales-formulas.md`*

### 17. How To Write An Amazing Sales Letter Fast

Assemble the letter from stacked parts; each part's only job is to pull readers to the next part.
*See: `sales-formulas.md`*

### 18. How To Write Killer Email Teasers Fast

An email's sole purpose is the click; sell curiosity, not the offer, and nail the subject line.
*See: `email-and-ads.md`*

### 19. The Hardest Draft You'll Ever Write

The first draft is the only hard part; write in chunks from a swipe file to beat the blank page.
*See: `swipe-and-polish.md`*

### 20. Make 'Em More Thirsty

Stories create desire and sales copy directs it; use myths, misconceptions, and mistakes to build thirst.
*See: `psychology-and-emotion.md`*

## Offers and psychology (Secrets 21 to 28)

### 21. Love Me, Hate Me, There's No Money In The Middle

Take a clear position; a strong persona attracts buyers on both sides and repels the indifferent middle.
*See: `psychology-and-emotion.md`*

### 22. "Oh Damn, I Got To Have That!"

Build a profitable promise from four parts: the hurdle, the prize, the timing, and the eliminator.
*See: `offers-and-desire.md`*

### 23. Put Lipstick On The Pig

Diagnose weak copy with a checklist before scrapping it; tighten headline, offer clarity, price, urgency, and proof.
*See: `review-checklist.md`*

### 24. Should I Join The Dark Side?

Use negatives ethically to meet people in their pain; never attack a person or company by name.
*See: `proof-and-closes.md`*

### 25. Stealth Closes

Sell without selling by delivering value, then pivoting with a soft "by the way" offer.
*See: `proof-and-closes.md`*

### 26. The Hired Gun

Audition copywriters on small jobs; you will still do the teaching and the final tweaking.
*See: `proof-and-closes.md`*

### 27. The Magic Desk

Use a guided empathy visualization to climb inside the prospect's mind and hear their WIIFM in their own words.
*See: `fred-avatar.md`*

### 28. The One And Only Purpose Of An Online Ad

An ad's only job is to make the right people click and the wrong people scroll on; lead with curiosity and a question.
*See: `email-and-ads.md`*

## Tools and refinement (Secrets 29 to 31)

### 29. You Can't Catch Fish Without A Hook

A hook is a one-sentence curiosity story (distinct from a USP) that pulls people into your offer.
*See: `headlines-and-bullets.md`*

### 30. Create Your Own Swipe File

Keep a swipe file of proven copy to warm up your brain and model patterns; add to it constantly.
*See: `swipe-and-polish.md`*

### 31. Polish Your Sales Copy

Proofread, test across browsers and devices, check the secondary reading path, and get a second pair of eyes.
*See: `swipe-and-polish.md`*
