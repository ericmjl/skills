# Headlines and Bullets Reference

> Frameworks adapted from Jim Edwards' Copywriting Secrets.

## Why the Headline Is 80% of the Copy's Success

Up to eight out of ten people who see a page or ad read the headline; only two out of ten read the body. A great headline with mediocre copy will outperform excellent copy with a weak headline. If the headline fails, nothing else gets read, the sales process never starts, and the rest of the work is wasted.

- Spend roughly half your total copywriting time on the headline alone. Do not treat it as an afterthought.
- The headline's only job is to stop the reader and make them start reading (or watching).
- Target emotion: a fear they want to avoid or a desire they want to fulfill. A great headline connects emotionally with the ideal prospect.
- A great headline can reduce total clicks while increasing click quality, because it filters out the wrong audience.
- Test headlines relentlessly. A single headline swap can move results from zero sales to five figures with the same traffic.

## Headline Patterns

Use these templates as fill-in-the-blank starting points. Keep timeframes believable, use odd numbers where possible, and always address a specific fear or desire.

### 1. How To (result)

Template: `How To [achieve desired result]`

- How To Get A Better Score On Your Next PT Test
- How To Get Rid Of Acne

### 2. How To (result) In (timeframe)

Template: `How To [achieve desired result] In As Little As [timeframe]`

- How To Double Your Pushups In As Little As 10 Days
- How To Get Rid Of Acne In As Little As 24 Hours

### 3. How To (result) Even If (objection)

Template: `How To [achieve desired result] In As Little As [timeframe] Even If [objection]`

- How To Pass Your PT Test In As Little As 2 Weeks Even If You Can't Do A Single Pull-Up Right Now
- How To Get Rid Of Your Pimples In As Little As 7 Days Even If You've Tried Everything Else And Failed

### 4. How Every (group) Can (result)

Template: `How Every [group] Can [achieve desired result]`

- How Every New Military Recruit Can Become A PT God In 12 Weeks
- How Every Teenager With Pimples Can Have Clearer Skin Fast

### 5. (Number) Quick And Easy Ways To (result)

Template: `[odd number] Quick And Easy Ways To [achieve desired result]`

- 5 Quick And Easy Ways To Max Out Your Pushups
- 5 Quick And Easy Ways To Get Rid Of Pimples

### 6. (Number) Ways To (result) And Avoid (negative)

Template: `[odd number] Fast Ways To Get [result] And Avoid [negative outcome]`

- 3 Fast Ways To Get A Better PT Test Score And Avoid The 'Fat Boy' PT Squad
- 3 Fast Ways To Get Rid Of Acne And Avoid Embarrassment

### 7. (Topic) Mistakes Every (group) Must Avoid

Template: `[number] [topic] Mistakes Every [group] Needs To Avoid`

- 3 PT Testing Mistakes Every Marine Needs To Avoid
- 5 Acne Treatment Mistakes Every Teenager Needs To Avoid

### 8. Warning: (fear-based hook)

Template: `WARNING: Don't Even Think About Trying To [do something] Until You Read This`

- WARNING: Don't Even Think About Trying To Pass Your Next PT Test Until You Read This
- WARNING: Here's What Every Teenager Needs To Know About Over-The-Counter Acne Treatments

Use warning headlines sparingly and realistically. Overuse kills credibility.

### 9. The Perfect Solution If You Want (result)

Template: `Here's The Perfect Solution If You Want [result] (Even If [objection])`

- Here's The Perfect Solution If You Want To Be A PT God (even if you can barely do 20 pushups right now)
- Here's The Perfect Solution If You Want Clearer Skin (even if you feel like there's no hope)

### 10. My Proven (method name) Method To (result)

Template: `My Proven "[method name]" Method To [achieve desired result]`

- My Proven "Pullup Push" Method To Double Your Pullups
- My Proven "Clear Skin" Method To Get Rid Of Your Pimples Forever

## The Ultimate Bullet Formula

Bullets are the workhorse of sales copy. They build curiosity, carry the weight of the message, and create pressure to act. A few excellent bullets outperform dozens of mediocre ones.

### Features vs Benefits vs Meaning

Master this three-level distinction before writing any bullet.

| Level | Definition | Example (cordless drill) |
|-------|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Feature** | What something IS | 18-volt battery |
| **Benefit** | What it DOES for you | Drill through hardwood without recharging every five minutes |
| **Deep benefit (meaning)** | How it makes you FEEL or what it means for your life | Finish the honey-do list in one afternoon instead of spreading it across three weekends |

People do not buy features. People buy benefits, and they buy hardest when you surface the meaning underneath.

### The Formula

Basic: `It [feature] so you can [benefit]`

Ultimate: `It [feature] so you can [benefit], which means [meaning]`

The "which means" clause is the secret sauce. It transforms a feature list into a desire-building machine.

### Worked Examples

Apply the ultimate formula to a set of wrenches:

- `One-piece set` so you can `keep all your wrenches together in one place`, which means `you'll never be left high and dry with the wrong wrench`.
- `SAE sizes` so you have `the exact size you need when you need it`, which means `you can get projects done faster and move on with your life`.
- `Chrome vanadium steel construction for strength and durability` so you can `work them hard`, which means `you'll spend a lot less money on tools over time`.
- `Stamped markings for easy size identification` so you can `quickly find the right size`, which means `no more pulling your hair out`.

Another example using an e-book (with page references to make benefits tangible):

- How to sell on Amazon (the Web's #1 e-book retailer WANTS you to upload your Kindle e-book for you, here's how) (Page 14)
- The sure-fire secret to creating an e-book that sells like crazy while having more fun than you ever thought possible (Page 23)
- How to quickly avoid the #1 mistake authors make that causes them to take months to write a book so you can finish in just a few days (Page 7)

## How Many Bullets To Use

Prefer four to twelve excellent bullets over fifty mediocre ones. If you need five or six final bullets, write ten to twelve and keep the best. Your first bullet is rarely as strong as your fifth or sixth; momentum improves quality.

## Where To Place Bullets

Use bullets wherever you need to build curiosity or summarize value.

- **Under the headline** of a sales letter or landing page: two or three bullets to pull readers into the body.
- **In the product description** on your site, on Amazon, or in a brochure: list the benefits (with meaning) of what they are buying.
- **In email teasers**: bullets preview what is inside and give reasons to click through.
- **In video descriptions and blog posts**: bullets summarize what people will see or read.
- **Anywhere you need to give people a reason to keep reading or to decide now**.

Bullets carry most of the weight of your copy once the headline has pulled the reader in. Treat them with the same care as the headline.
