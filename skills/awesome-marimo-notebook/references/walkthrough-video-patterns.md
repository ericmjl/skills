# Walkthrough Video Patterns

Patterns observed in effective notebook walkthrough videos. Use these to script
a demo video that showcases an interactive notebook's key moments.

## Common patterns of effective walkthroughs

### 1. Personal intro + one-sentence thesis
Open with name + paper/topic + one-line framing. Get to the point fast — no
preamble, no throat-clearing.

> "Hey everyone, I'm [name]. I'll walk you through this notebook exploring
> [paper/topic] — the core idea is [one sentence]."

### 2. Demo-driven — narrate the notebook live
Don't read markdown prose. Scroll/click through the notebook and narrate what's
on screen — point at things, describe what happens, interpret results in
real-time. The viewer should feel like they're looking over your shoulder.

### 3. One key interactive is the centerpiece
Identify THE widget that makes the core idea tangible. Spend the most time on
it (1-2 min) — let the viewer see it respond to interaction. Briefly cover the
rest of the notebook afterward.

### 4. Bridge: metaphor -> the real claim
After the demo interaction, explicitly connect the intuition to the paper's
actual claim:

> "But what if I told you this is what actually happens in [real scenario]..."

This is the moment the metaphor earns its place.

### 5. Extension clearly flagged
Call out your original contribution explicitly:

> "Here is where we go beyond the paper..." / "My first extension is..."

Cite a number if you have one. Viewers should be able to identify what YOU
added vs what the paper already contained.

### 6. Brief, gracious close
Short, warm, no recap:

> "Hope you enjoyed playing around with the notebook... thanks for watching!"
> "I hope you enjoy clicking through it... thanks for the opportunity to share."

### 7. Mention the tools
If the notebook used notable tools (marimo pair-programming, molab, specific
libraries), mention them briefly. It helps the ecosystem and signals craft.

## What varies by style

| Aspect | Long-form | Short-form |
|---|---|---|
| **Length** | 10-13 min — walks through every section | 3-5 min — highlights only |
| **Depth** | Full experiment walkthrough + numbers | Surface-level, experience-focused |
| **Tone** | Confident, pedagogical | Casual, fun |
| **Numbers cited** | Yes (metrics, hit rates) | Minimal or none |
| **Extension** | Clearly flagged + quantified | Framed as a creative device |

Both styles work. Short-form tends to be more shareable; long-form is more
thorough. Match the length to your audience.

## Script template (10 steps)

1. **Name + topic + one-sentence thesis** (10 seconds).
2. **Demo the key interactive** — narrate clicking through it (1-2 min).
3. **Bridge to the real claim** — "this is what actually happens in..."
4. **Flag YOUR extension** — "here is where we..." and cite a number.
5. **Briefly hit the other sections** — chart, experiment, close (1 min).
6. **Mention the GPU** if the notebook uses real compute.
7. **Warm, short close** — thanks, invitation to explore.
8. **~5 min, ~500-550 words** at a relaxed pace (~110 wpm). Don't read
   markdown — narrate the demo.
9. **Conversational connectors**: "let's see", "as you can see", "what if I
   told you", "here is where..."
10. **Mention the tools** that made it possible.
